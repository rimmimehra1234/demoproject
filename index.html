Which security camera should I choose?

Installing CCTV cameras is an effective way to measure and increase the security of your business site. Some examples are below:

Dome Camera

Small with a rounded dome and robust
structure design, dome CCTV cameras have
high quality and clear imaging against a
strong backlight and have a wider view
range than traditional security cameras.
Other than being more conspicuous than
traditional cameras, they are also water and
dust resistant.

Bullet Camera

A good robust structure design sporting a
tubular shape and instantly recognisable on
the side of a building, bullet cameras have
high-quality imaging against a strong
backlight. They are also water and dust
resistant, and exceptionally good for
general-purpose recording.

Fisheye Camera

Ideal for getting clear visibility over a large
open plan building, including warehouses
and car parks. Fisheye cameras offer
panoramic views or 360-degree views of
their surroundings. Water-resistant and
vandal-proof, fisheye cameras are robust
security cameras.

Pan-Tilt-Zoom Camera

The pan-tilt-zoom (PTZ) cameras work by
moving in different directions either
manually or autonomously to get a whole
picture of a large area. The CCTV camera
can be operated in real-time or on a
schedule and allows the camera to turn and
track. This makes it ideal for following a
target.

Network Video Recorder (NVR)

The network video recorder (NVR)
processes and stores the video information
received from CCTV security cameras. This
is the central storage device where footage
is kept and is where you would download
footage. This can be complemented with
cloud storage options and the ability to
export to DVD.
